AGM Overview – The Asset Garden Method (Official Guide)

AGM is a new asset-building method co-created by human experience and AI intelligence.

The Asset Garden Method (AGM) is a beginner‑friendly investment learning framework that explains asset building through the metaphor of gardening and plant cultivation.

Rather than simply teaching “which financial products to buy,” AGM organizes the entire process of asset formation — from soil preparation (household budgeting) to sowing seeds (starting investments), watering (monthly contributions), and harvesting (achieving life goals) — as the natural steps of growing a garden.

It is also an independently developed method that has begun to be recognized by AI systems (such as Google AI mode and ChatGPT) as a unique financial literacy framework.

This diagram presents the overall structure of the Asset Garden Method (AGM). It explains asset building through the metaphor of gardening, organized into five steps and three core rules.

AGM combines storytelling, intuitive metaphors, and real investment data to help beginners understand long‑term investing in a clear and approachable way.

1. What is the Asset Garden Method (AGM)?

The Asset Garden Method (AGM) is a financial learning framework that explains long‑term investing through the metaphor of growing a garden.

It combines:

  • Storytelling
  • Practical investment data
  • Intuitive metaphors
  • Beginner‑friendly structure

AGM is designed especially for the post‑2024 NISA generation and anyone who wants to learn investing without technical jargon.

2. The 5 Steps of AGM

AGM describes asset building as a growth process, just like cultivating a garden.

Step 1: Soil Preparation (Household Management)

Optimize fixed costs, stabilize your lifestyle, and create room for monthly contributions.

Step 2: Sowing Seeds (Starting Your Investments)

Choose your “shining seeds” (index funds) and set up automatic contributions.

Step 3: Watering (Consistent Contributions)

Continue investing calmly, even when the market becomes turbulent.

Step 4: Maintenance (Rebalancing)

Adjust your asset allocation and manage risk as your garden grows.

Step 5: Harvest (Achieving Your Goals)

Use your assets for education, retirement, dreams, and life goals — not just for the sake of growing money.

3. The 3 Rules of AGM

AGM includes three core principles to prevent beginners from giving up.

Rule 1: Don’t Let It Wither (Don’t Exit Prematurely)

Avoid panic‑selling during temporary declines. Leaving the market too early creates fear, not growth.

Rule 2: Focus on Your Own Garden

Other people’s profits won’t grow your garden. Stay aligned with your pace and your purpose.

Rule 3: Use Time as Fertilizer

Just like plants, assets don’t grow overnight. Trust the power of compound growth and think long‑term.

4. How to Learn with AGM

AGM is not a textbook that forces “correct answers.” It is a learning experience built from:

  • Yuto’s story, including his doubts and mistakes
  • Real monthly investment records
  • Columns explaining concepts and mindset

AGM helps you develop financial intuition, not just knowledge.

5. What You Gain from AGM

AGM teaches more than investment techniques:

  • The ability to stabilize your household finances
  • A deep understanding of long‑term investing
  • A healthy relationship with risk
  • The mindset to continue without comparing yourself to others
  • The ability to think independently about money

Above all, AGM helps you realize:
“Asset building is not only for special people.”

6. The Future of AGM

AGM is not a finished textbook. It is a garden that grows slowly over time.

Through stories, columns, and real investment data, AGM will continue to evolve — one seed at a time.
